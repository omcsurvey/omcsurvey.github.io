Sample Size & Margin of Error Analysis

Hepatitis-B KAP Study Among Medical Students in India

1. Sample Size Calculation

We calculated the required sample size using the following methodology:

Key Parameters:

  • Total Population (N): 560,560 students (112,112/year × 5 years) total number of students doing MBBS in India
  • Confidence Level: 95% (Z = 1.96)
  • Margin of Error (E): 5%
  • Expected Proportion (p): 0.5 (conservative estimate)

Calculation Formula:

\[ n = \frac{Z^2 \cdot p \cdot (1-p)}{E^2} \quad \text{and} \quad n_{\text{adjusted}} = \frac{n}{1 + \frac{n-1}{N}} \]

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Initial Calculation: \[ n = \frac{1.96^2 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.5}{0.05^2} = 384.16 \]
  2. Population Adjustment: \[ n_{\text{adjusted}} = \frac{384.16}{1 + \frac{383.16}{560,560}} \approx 384 \]

Final Determination:

We recommend a sample size of 385 participants to:

  • Maintain 95% confidence level
  • Achieve ±5% margin of error
  • Account for potential non-responses

2. Margin of Error Analysis (168 Responses)

After collecting 168 responses, we re-evaluated the margin of error:

Revised Parameters:

  • Collected Sample (n): 168
  • Population (N): 560,560
  • Confidence Level: 95%
  • Proportion (p): 0.5

Margin of Error Formula:

\[ E = Z \cdot \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{N-n}{N-1}} \]

Calculation Steps:

  1. Standard Error: \[ \sqrt{\frac{0.5 \cdot 0.5}{168}} \approx 0.0395 \]
  2. Correction Factor: \[ \sqrt{\frac{560,560 - 168}{560,560 - 1}} \approx 0.99985 \]
  3. Final Margin: \[ E = 1.96 \cdot 0.0395 \cdot 0.99985 \approx \mathbf{7.74\%} \]

Implications of 168 Responses:

  • Margin of Error: ±7.74% (vs original target of ±5%) this limit our reasearch power

Conclusion :

  • We clearly acknowledge limitations in methodology due to lack of response
  • If we could have get additional time to pursue additional data collection to reach target sample our research might be better
  • Still we hope our research is the founding frame work for further studies due to it's unique approaches
